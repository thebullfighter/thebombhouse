Amager Resource Centre

Waste-To-Energy

Construction of the new “Amager Resource Centre” (ARC) began in 2013. Prior to this, the old resource center was the most significant building in the area which was also home to all manner of Extreme Sports. In need of renewal, the proposal was a new Waste-to-Energy plant that is Economically, Environmentally and Socially profitable. The goal was to increase the relationship between the structure and the city by expanding the areas activities and turning the roof of the new ARC building into a ski slope. (http://www.big.dk/#projects-arc)

Extreme Sports

ARC: Waste Management. The plant converts waste into power for 62,500 homes and hot water for 160,000 homes. It cuts C02 emissions by 100,000 tonnes. The plant produces more clean water than it uses. Because of its filtration and other technologies, sulphur is expected to be reduced by 99.5% and Nitrogen Oxide by 90% (so what?). The special feature of the plant is its chimney which does NOT emit its exhaust continually, but instead “smoke rings” that consist of water vapour rather than actual smoke.
